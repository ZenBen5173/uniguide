# Scholarship & Financial Aid Application — Universiti Malaya

Source: UM HEPA Scholarship & Financial Aid Section, JPA Public Service Department portal, MARA scholarship division, MyBrainSc / KPT, Yayasan UM — `https://hep.um.edu.my/`, `https://esilav2.jpa.gov.my/`, `https://mybrainsc.mohe.gov.my/`, `https://www.mara.gov.my/`.

## Eligibility Overview

Eligibility for any scholarship at UM is filtered through five dimensions:

1. **Programme level** — undergraduate vs. postgraduate (taught vs. research). Different scholarships per level.
2. **Citizenship** — Malaysian (MY) vs. International (INTL). Most government and need-based scholarships are Malaysian-only.
3. **Race / ethnicity tier** (Bumiputera vs. non-Bumiputera) — affects eligibility for MARA, JPA cohort allocations, and certain quota-based schemes.
4. **CGPA tier**:
   - ≥ 3.70 — competitive merit scholarships
   - 3.30 – 3.69 — most maintenance scholarships
   - 3.00 – 3.29 — limited options, mainly need-based
   - < 3.00 — usually disqualified; exceptions for hardship cases via faculty appeal
5. **Family income tier (DOSM brackets)**:
   - **B40** — household income < RM 4,850/month
   - **M40** — RM 4,850 – RM 10,960/month
   - **T20** — > RM 10,960/month

B40 and lower-M40 students qualify for need-based bursaries; T20 generally qualifies only for merit-based.

## Available Scholarships

**UM-internal (apply via UM portal):**
- **Yayasan Universiti Malaya (Yayasan UM)** — need-based, UG + PG, Malaysian, prioritises B40/M40 students with CGPA ≥ 3.30.
- **IPS Bright Sparks** — UM Institute for Postgraduate Studies; for top postgraduate admits with CGPA ≥ 3.70 from undergraduate.
- **Tunku Abdul Rahman Scholarship** — UM merit-based, undergraduate.
- **UM Bursary** — small need-based grant for B40 students experiencing financial hardship mid-semester.

**Government scholarships:**
- **JPA Scholarship (Public Service Department)** — primarily for SPM/STPM leavers entering UG; also has JPA-PIDN for academic staff pursuing further studies. Apply via `esilav2.jpa.gov.my`.
- **MARA Scholarship** — Bumiputera only, UG + PG, Malaysian; need-based + merit. Apply via `mara.gov.my`.
- **MyBrainSc** — postgraduate research scholarship for Master's and PhD candidates in science and humanities. Apply via `mybrainsc.mohe.gov.my`.

**Corporate scholarships (competitive, merit-based):**
- **Yayasan Khazanah** — flagship competitive scholarship for top UG admits.
- **Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) Scholarship** — finance, economics, banking students.
- **Petronas Education Sponsorship Programme** — engineering, geosciences, technical fields.
- **Public Bank Scholarship** — broad fields; high CGPA requirement.
- **Sime Darby Scholarship** — broad fields; leadership emphasis.
- **Maybank GO Ahead. Scholarship** — broad fields; entrepreneurial profile.

## Document Checklist

The set of documents you need depends on which scholarships you apply for, but the common core is:

- Academic transcripts (latest semester + cumulative).
- Identity documents (MyKad / IC for Malaysians; passport for international).
- Parents' / guardians' MyKad.
- **Income proof** — one of: parents' EPF statement, parents' income tax form (LHDN BE/B), employer salary slip (3 months), or a letter from the village / penghulu (Surat Pengesahan Pendapatan) for informal-sector parents.
- Academic certificates (SPM / STPM / Matriculation / Foundation results).
- Motivation letter / personal statement (typically 500–1000 words).
- 2 recommendation letters (academic + character).
- For postgraduate applicants: research proposal + supervisor endorsement (research mode).

## Application Process

The process has the same shape regardless of scholarship:

1. **Eligibility filtering** — given your level / citizenship / CGPA / income, identify which scholarships you qualify for. Apply only to those — bulk-applying to ineligible ones wastes everyone's time.
2. **Document collection** — gather the core documents and any scholarship-specific extras.
3. **Application submission** — most via online portal (JPA, MARA, MyBrainSc, Khazanah). Some still require physical copies submitted to UM HEPA.
4. **Faculty endorsement** (if required) — Yayasan UM, IPS Bright Sparks need a faculty letter.
5. **Interview** (if shortlisted) — most competitive scholarships interview top candidates.
6. **Outcome** — offer letter or rejection. Some have appeal windows.
7. **Acceptance** — sign agreement, open designated bank account if required.

## Yayasan UM Pathway (UM-internal need-based)

**Eligibility:** Malaysian, currently registered UM student, CGPA ≥ 3.30, family income B40 or lower-M40.

**Documents:** transcripts, parent income proof, MyKad, family financial declaration, motivation letter.

**Process:** apply via UM HEPA portal → faculty endorsement → Yayasan UM committee review → outcome within 4–6 weeks.

**Renewal:** maintain CGPA ≥ 3.30 each semester. CGPA drop triggers a review meeting; one warning before scholarship is revoked.

## JPA / MARA Pathway (government)

**JPA Scholarship for Further Studies (postgraduate):**
- Eligibility: Malaysian, government employees or top-tier graduates, CGPA ≥ 3.50.
- Apply via `esilav2.jpa.gov.my` during the annual application window (usually April–May).
- Bonded service: typically 2 years per year of sponsored study.

**MARA Scholarship (Bumiputera only):**
- Eligibility: Bumiputera, Malaysian, CGPA ≥ 3.00 (varies per scheme).
- Apply via `mara.gov.my` portal.
- Bonded service for some schemes; review terms carefully.

## MyBrainSc Pathway (postgraduate research)

**Eligibility:** Malaysian, postgraduate research mode (Master's or PhD), in science / humanities fields prioritised by KPT.

**Process:**
1. Secure UM postgraduate admission first (with supervisor confirmed).
2. Apply for MyBrainSc separately via `mybrainsc.mohe.gov.my`.
3. Supervisor endorsement required.
4. KPT panel review.
5. Outcome announcement (annual cycle).

**Stipend:** monthly allowance + tuition coverage. Renewable subject to research progress.

## Corporate Scholarships

For T20 / high-CGPA students, corporate scholarships are the main path. Process is competitive:

- Online application + essays.
- Aptitude tests (some — e.g., Khazanah).
- Multi-round interview (assessment centre, panel, final).
- Bonded service with the sponsor (typically 3–7 years post-graduation).

**Critical:** application windows are narrow (often Feb–April for the September intake). Late applications are rejected without exception.

## Postgraduate Funding Alternative — Graduate Research Assistant Scheme

Postgraduate students who don't secure a scholarship can apply for the **UM Graduate Research Assistant (GRA) Scheme** — paid RA position under a supervisor with funded research grants. Apply directly to the supervisor; funding sourced from their grant budget. Pay: typically RM 1,800 – 2,500/month.

## Renewal & Maintenance

Most scholarships require:
- **CGPA ≥ 3.30** maintained each semester (some require 3.50).
- **Full-time registration** — taking too few credits triggers review.
- **Annual progress report** for postgraduate research scholarships.
- **No academic misconduct or disciplinary action.**

A single semester below the CGPA threshold typically triggers a **warning + review meeting**. Two consecutive semesters → scholarship revoked.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Don't know which they're eligible for** — students often apply to wrong scholarships, get auto-rejected, miss the right one's deadline.
- **Income proof confusion** — parents in informal sector (farmers, traders, gig workers) struggle to produce income proof; the Surat Pengesahan Pendapatan from village head is often refused by reviewers if format is wrong.
- **Bumiputera / non-Bumiputera assumptions** — non-Bumiputera students sometimes apply for MARA without realising it's restricted; vice versa, eligible Bumiputera students don't realise they qualify for additional schemes.
- **Bond confusion** — students accept JPA / corporate scholarships without understanding the multi-year bonded service — leaving early triggers full repayment with interest.
- **Renewal fail** — CGPA threshold for renewal is stricter than for application; many lose their scholarship in their second year.
- **Application portal fragmentation** — JPA, MARA, MyBrainSc, Khazanah, UM all on different portals with different deadlines; no consolidated calendar.
- **PTPTN confusion** — PTPTN is a *loan*, not a scholarship; many students don't realise this until repayment notices arrive after graduation.
- **Postgraduate students missing the GRA route** — those who fail MyBrainSc don't realise they can ask their supervisor for a GRA position funded from research grants.
